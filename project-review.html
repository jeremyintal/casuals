Hypothesis

NBA fans already love trade trees. Casuals turns that behavior into a daily scoreboard.

Core Bet

There is a repeatable daily habit hiding inside NBA Twitter's existing "how did this team turn a random asset into a star?" conversations. Add scarcity, a shot clock, identity tiers, and a spoiler-safe share result, and the behavior can become a Wordle-shaped game.

Why It Spreads

The share is a personal flex first and an ad second. A result like SICKO or CERTIFIED CASUAL gives players status, shame, and a reason to challenge friends who think they know ball.

Problem It Solves

The NBA has rich transaction lore, but no simple daily ritual for proving you know it.

For fans

The "sicko" knowledge is scattered across Reddit threads, tweets, Basketball-Reference pages, and memory. Casuals packages it into a two-minute playable challenge.

For sharing

NBA trade trivia is usually spoiled by explaining it. Casuals lets players share a score, tier, time, and endpoint challenge without revealing the answers.

For retention

The reveal is content: win or lose, players leave with a memorable trade story they can repeat. That makes the end screen part of the product, not just a result modal.

Product Goals

Make a daily game that feels like playing GM under a shot clock.

Experience goals

  • One daily chain, shared by everyone, rolling over at midnight Eastern.
  • Six possessions, 24-second clock, turnover reset to 14, violation reveals a hint.
  • Autocomplete answers, no spelling punishment, and multi-answer trade returns.
  • End sheet with full transaction recap, score, tier stamp, and spoiler-safe sharing.

Business/product goals

  • Build a launch runway of at least 60 verified puzzles.
  • Target strong daily retention and a 15%+ share rate.
  • Use group chats, NBA Twitter, Reddit, and daily reveals as the growth loop.
  • Delay accounts, leaderboards, squads, and native apps until the core loop and content supply are strong.

Known Gaps And Risks

What is still not launch-ready

Launch blockers
  • Answers ship in the client bundle; public launch needs server-side answer validation.
  • Only 9 puzzles are shipped; the plan needs roughly 60 verified puzzles for launch runway.
  • No automated tests yet for engine state transitions, rollover, stats, or sharing.
  • Third-level hangman hint is not implemented; second violation repeats hint 2.
Product risks
  • Data credibility is the moat and the biggest failure mode: one wrong chain can damage trust.
  • Generated candidates are unverified by design; curation cannot be skipped.
  • Draft-day chains still require manual work because BBRef transaction pages do not include draft selections.
  • Web Share API and native share behavior still need real-device checks.
What's Next
